HIV CASES - Philippines Faces Swift Rise in HIV Cases; Youth Hit Hard—Government Eyes Emergency Action

The Philippines now records 57 new HIV cases daily, with a 500% increase among 15–25-year-olds since 2010. The Department of Health and UN agencies are urging a national public health emergency, citing poor sex education and risky online behavior. Most new cases are male, with a third coming from the youth sector. Authorities call for urgent action in testing, education, and treatment access.

DOH: Dengue Situation in PH Still Manageable, But Public Advised

The Department of Health reports that despite a spike in dengue cases—over 95,000 recorded from January to April 2025—the case fatality rate remains low (around 0.36%), and recent weeks have shown a 23% drop in new infections. Officials attribute the decline to intensified mosquito-control campaigns, "Dengue Fast Lanes," and effective community cleanup drives, while urging the public to keep water sources clear and seek early consultation.
